Frequently Asked Questions

How do I log my food?

Tap the Food tab at the bottom → tap + Add next to any meal → search for your food in the database (190+ Indian foods). You can search in Hindi or English.

I can't find a specific food. What do I do?

Type the food name in the search box. If it's not in our database, tap "Ask AI: Get nutrition for [food]" — our AI will look up the nutrition info for you.

How are my calorie targets calculated?

FitSutra uses the Mifflin-St Jeor formula based on your age, weight, height, gender, and activity level. We then subtract 500 kcal for fat loss (creating a healthy ~0.5 kg/week deficit).

Why is my water goal higher today?

FitSutra adjusts your water goal based on local temperature. In hot weather (above 30°C), your goal increases by 20-40% to compensate for increased sweat loss. The temperature chip on the dashboard shows the current adjustment.

The water reminders aren't ringing. Why?

Make sure: (1) Notifications are enabled for FitSutra in your phone settings, (2) Your wake/sleep times in Water → Settings cover the current time, (3) You haven't already reached your daily water goal.

Can I use FitSutra without internet?

Yes! FitSutra works offline as a Progressive Web App. Your data syncs to the cloud when you're back online.

Why does my streak show 1 day when I've been using the app for weeks?

The streak counts consecutive days where you logged food, exercise, or weight. If you skipped a day without logging anything, the streak resets. Open the app daily and log at least one item to maintain your streak.

How do I change my goal weight?

Go to Profile → tap your weight info → update your target weight. The app will recalculate your daily calorie target automatically.

Is my data safe?

Yes. All data is encrypted in transit (HTTPS) and at rest (Firebase). We never sell or share your data with third parties. See our Privacy Policy for details.

How do I switch between Hindi and English?

Tap the "EN" / "हिंदी" button at the top right of the dashboard to toggle between languages.
